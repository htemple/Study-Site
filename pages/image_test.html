JPGs

JPG images are good for photos, but they can't handle transparency! Use a .png instead maybe.

  • Animations: NO
  • Transparency: NO
  • Color pallete: LIMITED

GIFs

GIFs are good for animations. Their pixels are either transparent or not, binary style. Their color pallete is also limited, making photos NOT a good idea.

  • Animations: YES
  • Transparency: BINARY
  • Color pallete: LIMITED

PNGs

PNGs are good for diagrams and icons. They are bigger than a jpg, but hold the same quality to the naked eye. However, they don't have color problems and can handle transparency!

  • Animations: NO
  • Transparency: FULL SPECTRUM
  • Color pallete: FULL

SVGs

SVGs are Scalable Vector Graphics files. They can scale up or down without losing quality. In other words, you won't see any nasty pixels when making the image bigger. They are good to use whenever you can, but be wary of file sizes. They are best for nice graphics or logos.

  • Animations: NO
  • Transparency: FULL SPECTRUM
  • Color pallete: FULL

Extra note: There is one potential issue with SVGs: for them to display consistently across browsers, you need to convert any text fields to outlines using your image editor (e.g., Abode Illustrator or Sketch). If your images contain a lot of text (like the fancy screenshots in this tutorial), this can have a big impact on file size.
